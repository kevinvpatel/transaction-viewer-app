Eligibility Required to Open PMJDY Account

To open a PMJDY account, you must meet the below-given eligibility criteria.

  • Only Indian citizens can open an account under this scheme
  • In order to open an account under the PMJDY, individuals can visit any nearest bank branch or Business Correspondent (Bank Mitr)
  • Minors who are over the age of 10 can open the savings account at a bank branch.
  • The account opening form of the scheme is available in both English and Hindi format, on the PMJDY's official site. 

What are the Documents Required to Open PMJDY Account?

You must carry the below listed documents to open PMJDY account under the scheme.

  • Driving licence
  • Voter ID
  • Passport
  • PAN Card
  • Job card issued by the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act
  • An attested passport size photograph along with a letter from a Gazetted officer must be submitted
  • Any Identity card generated by the state or Central Government, Scheduled Commercial Banks, Regulatory or Statutory.

Mission Mode Objectives of Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana 

PMJDY, to be conducted in Mission Mode, foresees the development of reliable financial services within a reasonable distance for all people. This contains some elements which are listed below. 

Enabling the universal access of banking facilities: Filtering each district into a Sub-Service Area (SSA) catering for 1000-1500 dwellings in a way that every dwelling has access to banking facilities within a fair range. 

Offering essential banking accounts with RuPay debit card and overdraft facilities to every household: The aim would be to eventually provide all uncovered residences with bank services by developing basic bank accounts by August 2015. RuPay Debit Card will be issued to account holder. Overdraft facility to every basic banking account holder would be provided after a proper evaluation. 

Financial Literacy Programme: Financial literacy would be an important aspect of the project to enable beneficiaries to make the most use of the financial services they access. 

Establishment of Credit Guarantee Fund: Development of a credit guarantee fund would compensate the defaults in overdraft accounts. 

Micro-insurance: To enable both willing and eligible persons with micro-insurance on an on-going basis.  

Insurance Cover Under Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana

On 15th August, 2014 the honourable Prime Minister unveiled a robust Financial Inclusion Plan addressing the vast number of citizens presently depriving people of basic financial services.  The Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) intends to deliver a basic bank account to every household who don’t have or carry a bank account. The bank account is bundled with a RuPay debit card with a built-in accidental cover of Rs 1 lakh for every family. At the announcement of the PMJDY scheme on 28.08.14 in New Delhi, the Honourable Prime Minister declared a Rs. 30,000/- life cover with the RuPay Card for all those who first register to a bank account during the period from 15th August 2014 to 26th January 2015. This life insurance cover under Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana will cover the death of the life assured, for any cause.  The policy aims at ensuring coverage for those households who can not afford direct insurance,, including the urban and rural families who are not covered under any life insurance benefits or scheme. 

Eligibility required

As per the following eligibility criteria one can get life cover of Rs. 30,000/- in case of death due to any cause. 

  • For the first time, the individual opening the bank account, in addition to RuPay Card, during the duration from 15-08-14 to 26-01-15, or any additional duration may be further extended by the Govt of India.
  • The person must usually be the head of the household or an earning member of the family and should be in the 18-59 age group. In case the head of the family is 60 years of age or older, the second earning member of the family will be covered, subject to eligibility, in the above listed age bracket.
  • Individual must have a RuPay Card and Bio – Metric Card registered to a bank account
  • Any bank account including a small account managed by an individual is eligible.
  • The above RuPay Card should be valid and being used for the coverage to be effective. 
  • Under the Bima Scheme, only one person in the family will be covered and in the case of the individual with several cards / accounts the coverage will be provided only under one card i.e. one individual per household will earn a single cover of Rs.30,000/-, according to the criteria of eligibility.
  • The insurance cover of Rs 30.000/- under the policy would initially be for a 5-year term. The policy will then be updated, and the terms and conditions of its operation, including the concern of the insured's future premium payment afterwards, will be appropriately determined. 

Claim Settlement

The nominee of the account holder is eligible to claim the amount of Rs.30,000/-. The Rs.30000/- compensation balance is owed to the account holder's nominee(s). The risk cover will be given to the individual from the age of 18 (Completed) before he meets the completed age of 60 years. And after the 60 years of age he / she can exit the scheme. The account holder's nominee will be eligible to receive Rs.30,000/-'death compensation in case of the account holder's unfortunate death related to any circumstance. And one can exit the scheme after reaching age 60 years old. 

The operation of claim settlement will be transferred to the LIC Offices. The further process will be as follows:

  • Claim papers will be obtained by the appropriate Bank's District Branch / Nodal Office and sent to LIC's Pension & Group Scheme Units for settling claims. 
  • The claim will be paid out in the bank account of the nominee
  • The amount of the claim will be transferred to the nominee's bank account by NEFT / APBS.

Features of the PMJDY scheme

Some of the key benefits of the scheme are mentioned below:

  • Interest is provided for the contributions made towards the scheme
  • No minimum balance is required to maintain by an individual
  • An overdraft facility is given in case individuals hold the account for 6 months
  • Individuals can earn Rs.1 lakh Cover for Accidental Benefits under the RuPay program.
  • Insurance benefits and access to pensions are offered under the scheme.
  • Direct Benefit Transfer method is given in case individuals become beneficiaries of government schemes.
  • An overdraft facility of Rs.5,000 is issued per household
  • The cover for Personal Accident can only be asserted after an effective non-financial or financial transaction has been produced by the RuPay Cardholder. Under the scheme, transactions made within 90 days of the incident are considered as liable PMJDY transactions. The transaction must therefore be rendered at an E-COM, POS, ATM, Bank Mitra, branch of the bank, etc. 
  • Account-holders can also use the Mobile banking service to verify their balance.

Current Milestone of Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana

Under the country's flagship financial inclusion project Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY), initiated by the Modi government about six years ago, now has 40 Crore active bank accounts. There are 40,05 crore beneficiaries of this scheme according to the latest statistic and deposits in Jan Dhan bank accounts surpass Rs 1.30 lac crore. Another achievement reached under the world's biggest initiative for financial inclusion, PMJDY: Cumulative accounts opened under the scheme reach 40 Cr mark. The milestone arrives just ahead of the announcement of PMJDY 's 6th anniversary. The scheme was initiated on 28 August 2014 with the goal of providing the citizens of the country with equal access to banking facilities. Accounts launched under PMJDY are Basic Savings Bank Deposit (BSBD) accounts that have added RuPay debit card and overdraft functionality. Maintaining a minimum balance is not required in BSBD accounts. 

In consideration of the scheme's stunning success, the government expanded the accident insurance coverage to Rs 2 lakh in 2018, from Rs 1 lakh for new accounts launched after August 28, 2018. In addition, the cap facility for overdraft was doubled to Rs 10,000 too. The Government has also changed the attention from "every family" to "every unbanked citizen."
